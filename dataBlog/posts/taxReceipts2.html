Where the UK’s £955bn of tax comes from 2025–26

Every UK tax in the 2025–26 tax year, as nested rings. Click a segment to zoom in; click the centre, the outside, or Reset to zoom out, and hover for detail. Switch Who pays to regroup every tax by whether it falls on individuals or companies.

Data: UK tax receipts for the 2025–26 year, compiled by Tax Policy Associates (source dataset), in £ millions. Rings are sized by receipts; categories group taxes by economic incidence rather than collecting body. A few series are modelled splits (income tax by source, gaming & stamp sub-totals); petroleum revenue tax is a net repayment and shows as zero area. The Who pays view is an inferred attribution of each tax to the party that ultimately bears it — consumption taxes to individuals, profit/employer/business levies to companies — and is a simplification (many taxes fall partly on both). · Built with D3 by Daniel Lewis.
