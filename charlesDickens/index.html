Six numbers issued. Six numbers to follow. A cathedral town, an opium den, a vanished young man, and a choirmaster you would not leave alone with your nephew. The author regrets that, owing to circumstances entirely beyond his control, the solution has been placed permanently on back-order. You decide who did it. Everyone else has.

Great Expectations

The reader's favourite

"My father's family name being Pirrip, and my Christian name Philip…"

A blacksmith's boy, a convict on the marshes, a wedding-cake gone to the spiders, and the discovery that a gentleman is made of something other than money. Two endings supplied; the author was talked into the kinder one by Bulwer-Lytton, and has heard about it ever since.

David Copperfield

The author's favourite child

"Whether I shall turn out to be the hero of my own life…"

Of all his books, the one he liked best — and the one with the most of himself in it, right down to the reversed initials. Contains the Micawber Principle of personal finance, still unimproved upon by any economist since.

A Christmas Carol

A ghost story of Christmas

"Marley was dead: to begin with."

Written in six weeks of furious night-walking, priced too low, bound too handsomely, and it barely made its author a penny — while making the fortune of Christmas itself. If you keep the day well, you learned how here.

The Question Beneath All The Others

Are there no prisons? Are there no workhouses?

Scrooge asks it as a defence. The Ghost returns it as an indictment. Every book on this site puts some form of the same question, and it has not expired with the century that raised it: when we build institutions to hold the poor at arm's length — the workhouse, the debtors' prison, the Court of Chancery, the Circumlocution Office — whom are they really built to comfort?

The author's answer was never a pamphlet's answer. It was Jo the crossing-sweeper, who was moved on until he died of it. It was the Marshalsea, where his own father was locked up for a debt of forty pounds while a twelve-year-old Charles pasted labels on blacking-pots. It was two children under a spirit's robe — the boy Ignorance, the girl Want — with a warning to beware the boy most of all. He did not ask you to feel guilty. He asked you to look. Then he made it impossible not to.

Household Words — the weekly miscellany

Fiction, journalism, sanitary outrage, and Christmas numbers, delivered every Saturday. "Familiar in their mouths as HOUSEHOLD WORDS." Should the conductor quarrel with his publishers — and he will — you shall be transferred, without interruption, to All the Year Round.

No purchase necessary except the three-halfpence. We shall never sell your particulars — we leave that sort of thing to Mr Casby, who screws it out of Bleeding Heart Yard.
