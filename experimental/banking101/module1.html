Module 1: The Evolved Front-to-Back Operating Model and Modern Architecture

Establishing the foundational paradigm of modern banking architecture, characterizing the transition from monolithic legacy systems to decoupled, cloud-native infrastructures.

Reducing Technical Debt & Dependencies

The structural evolution of banking architecture is designed to reduce technical debt, eliminate dependencies on specialized legacy coding skills (e.g., COBOL), and facilitate real-time decision-making across the enterprise.

A contemporary front-to-back platform serves as a unified foundation for all investments and operations. This encompasses the seamless integration of:

  • Order Management Systems (OMS)
  • Execution Management Systems (EMS)
  • Portfolio Management
  • Post-Trade Operations
  • Compliance and Enterprise Data Management

Fluid Movement Without Batch Processing

The overarching goal of front-to-back modernization is to ensure that investment decisions move fluidly through execution, allocation, compliance, and post-trade processing without reliance on manual spreadsheets or outdated end-of-day batch processing.

Topological Transformation of the Middle & Back Offices

Decoupled banking systems, supported by process mining and generative AI, are actively automating process reconciliation, exception handling, and regulatory reporting.

Generative AI in the SDLC

Within the software development lifecycle (SDLC), the platform integration of generative AI is actively reducing manual coding efforts by 10% to 50%. This efficiency inversion fundamentally changes how quickly banking products are deployed and drastically reduces traditional technology development costs.
