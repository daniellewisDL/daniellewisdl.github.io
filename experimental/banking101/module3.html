Module 3: Investment Banking, Global Markets, and Asset Management

Exploring the investment banking ecosystem landscape undergoing an "innovation supercycle" that fundamentally alters capital formation, M&A, and strategies across public and private markets.

The Restructuring of Capital Markets and M&A

Mergers and acquisitions are driven by the alignment of capital availability in public and private markets, alongside a resurgence in IPOs. A critical component is the rise of activist investor campaigns, which have reached a five-year high.

Tactics employed by activists include:

  • Demands for urgent corporate sale processes rather than prolonged self-help plans.
  • The aggressive return of capital to shareholders.
  • The forced separation of misaligned or underperforming business units.

The Rise of Private Credit

Heightened regulations following the global financial crisis led traditional banks to retrench from middle-market lending. This opened the door for non-bank lenders and direct lending platforms (private credit) to become an essential source of corporate financing, particularly for private equity-backed companies throughout their lifecycles.

The Rebound of Active Management and Hedge Funds

The asset management industry sits at a critical inflection point where the commoditization of passive, long-only funds clashes with the AI-enabled resurgence of active management. Boundaries between public and private markets are blurring (e.g., active ETFs and hybrid vehicles).

Hedge Fund Resurgence

The hedge fund industry has fully rebounded from the stagnation of the 2010s, surpassing $5 trillion in AUM and consistently delivering double-digit returns with over 300 basis points of annualized alpha since the early 2020s.

Macroeconomic mechanics: A transition from a highly correlated, low-dispersion environment to a regime characterized by low stock-to-stock correlations, high dispersion, and elevated volatility—mathematically conducive to skilled alpha generation.

Despite growth, the investable universe has massively expanded (global equities up ~150%, private credit up ~250%), ensuring ample capacity for capital deployment.
