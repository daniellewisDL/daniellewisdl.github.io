Module 6: Trade Finance, Supply Chain Digitization, and DLT

From proofs-of-concept to production-grade digital infrastructure, eliminating legacy paper-based vulnerability and structural inefficiencies.

Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) and Digital Trade

The rising threat of generative AI being used maliciously to forge warehouse receipts or duplicate invoices has prompted the transition to cryptographic proof of asset provenance.

Global legal frameworks are falling into place:

  • United Kingdom: The Electronic Trade Documents Act enables live transactions with electronic bills of lading (eBLs) and digital promissory notes.
  • Germany & France: Expanding electronic securities frameworks and clarifying definitions of "reliable systems" for digital originals.

Solving the "Swivel Chair" Problem

Traditional trade finance was plagued by the back-office necessity of manually cross-referencing data between legacy systems, shipping registries, and compliance databases.

AI-driven document checking and autonomous compliance tools directly address this inefficiency. Advanced analytics map complex trade flows against rapidly expanding unilateral sanctions, ensuring stringent due diligence without paralyzing trade velocity.
