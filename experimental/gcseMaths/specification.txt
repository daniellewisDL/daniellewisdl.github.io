3. Ratio, Proportion and Rates of Change
Units and Scale: Change freely between standard and compound units (e.g., speed, rates of pay, density, pressure) and use scale factors, diagrams, and maps
.
Ratio Applications: Express quantities as fractions of another, reduce ratios to simplest form, divide quantities into part:part or part:whole ratios, and apply to context (e.g., conversion, scaling, mixing)
.
Proportion: Express multiplicative relationships, understand proportion as equality of ratios, relate ratios to linear functions, and solve direct/inverse proportion problems graphically and algebraically
.
Percentages: Define percentage as parts per hundred, express quantities as percentages, handle values >100%, and solve percentage change, original value, and simple/compound interest problems
.
Similarity and Scale Factors: Compare lengths, areas, and volumes using ratio notation, making links to similarity and scale factors
.
Inverse Proportion Equations: Understand that X inversely proportional to Y means X is proportional to 1/Y and interpret these equations
.
Rates of Change Graphs: Interpret straight-line gradients as rates of change; Higher tier students must also interpret instantaneous rates of change (gradients of tangents/chords) on curves
.
Growth and Decay: Set up, solve, and interpret growth and decay problems; Higher tier students must also work with general iterative processes
.
4. Geometry and Measures
Properties and Definitions: Use conventional terms for points, lines, polygons, and symmetries; derive properties of specific quadrilaterals (square, rectangle, parallelogram, trapezium, kite, rhombus) and triangles
.
Constructions and Loci: Use ruler and compass to construct perpendicular bisectors, perpendiculars from/at a point, and angle bisectors, and use these to solve loci problems
.
Angle Facts: Apply properties of angles at a point, on a straight line, vertically opposite, alternate/corresponding angles on parallel lines, and sum of angles in triangles and polygons
.
Congruence and Similarity: Use basic triangle congruence criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS) and apply similarity and Pythagoras' theorem to obtain simple proofs
.
Transformations: Construct and identify similar/congruent shapes through rotation, reflection, translation, and enlargement using fractional scale factors; Higher tier students must also use negative scale factors and describe changes achieved by combinations of transformations
.
Circles: Identify definitions (centre, radius, chord, diameter, circumference, tangent, arc, sector, segment); Higher tier students must also apply and prove standard circle theorems concerning angles, radii, tangents, and chords
.
3D Shapes: Identify properties (faces, edges, vertices) of cubes, cuboids, prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones, and spheres, and construct/interpret plans and elevations
.
Mensuration: Measure lines/angles, use bearings, and calculate area (triangles, parallelograms, trapezia), volume (cuboids, right prisms, cylinders), circumference/area of circles, and perimeter
.
Advanced Mensuration: Calculate arc lengths, sector areas, and surface area/volume of spheres, pyramids, cones, and composite solids. Apply relationships between lengths in similar figures; Higher tier students must also apply relationships between areas and volumes in similar figures
.
Trigonometry: Use Pythagoras' theorem and trigonometric ratios (sin,cos,tan) in right-angled 2D triangles, and know exact values for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60° (and 90° for sin/cos); Higher tier students must also apply these in 3D figures, general triangles, use the sine rule, cosine rule, and the area formula

Vectors: Describe translations as 2D vectors, apply addition, subtraction, scalar multiplication, and use column/diagrammatic representations; Higher tier students must also use vectors to construct geometric arguments and proofs

5. Probability
Frequencies and Experiments: Record, describe, and analyse frequency outcomes using tables/frequency trees, and apply ideas of randomness, fairness, and expected outcomes of multiple experiments
.
Probability Scales and Properties: Relate relative expected frequencies to theoretical probability on a 0-1 scale, and understand that probabilities of exhaustive or mutually exclusive events sum to one
.
Sampling: Understand that empirical unbiased samples tend towards theoretical probability distributions as sample size increases
.
Possibility Spaces and Events: Systematically enumerate sets/combinations using tables, grids, Venn diagrams, and tree diagrams, and construct theoretical possibility spaces to calculate probabilities for single and combined equally likely outcomes
.
Combined and Conditional Events: Calculate the probability of independent and dependent combined events; Higher tier students must also calculate and interpret conditional probabilities using expected frequencies, two-way tables, tree diagrams, and Venn diagrams
.
6. Statistics
Populations and Sampling: Infer properties of populations or distributions from a sample while knowing the limitations of sampling
.
Data Representation: Interpret and construct frequency tables, bar charts, pie charts, pictograms, vertical line charts, and time series line graphs
.
Continuous and Grouped Data: Construct and interpret histograms with equal/unequal class intervals and cumulative frequency graphs (Higher tier only)
.
Distribution Analysis: Interpret, analyse, and compare data sets using appropriate graphical representation and measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode, modal class) and spread (range, outliers); Higher tier students must also use box plots, quartiles, and inter-quartile range
.
Population Statistics and Bivariate Data: Apply statistics to describe a population, use and interpret scatter graphs, recognise correlation (understanding it doesn't indicate causation), draw lines of best fit, make predictions, and safely interpolate/extrapolate
.
