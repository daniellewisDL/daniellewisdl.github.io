3.1 Units & Scale Factors

💡 Key Concept

You must convert fluently between standard and compound units. A scale factor describes how many times larger (or smaller) one quantity is compared to another.

Compound Units

Speed = distance ÷ time (m/s, km/h, mph)

Density = mass ÷ volume (g/cm³, kg/m³)

Pressure = force ÷ area (N/m², Pa)

Rate of pay = pay ÷ time (£/hr)

✏️ Worked Example

Convert 72 km/h to m/s.

Step 1: 72 km = 72 000 m
Step 2: 1 hour = 3600 s
Step 3: 72 000 ÷ 3600 = 20 m/s ✓

Scale Diagrams & Maps

A map scale of 1 : 50 000 means 1 cm on the map = 50 000 cm = 500 m in real life.

3.2 Ratio Applications

💡 Key Concept

A ratio compares quantities. a : b means "a parts to b parts". Always simplify to the lowest terms by dividing by the HCF.

Dividing in a Given Ratio

✏️ Worked Example

Divide £120 in the ratio 3 : 5

Step 1: Total parts = 3 + 5 = 8
Step 2: One part = £120 ÷ 8 = £15
Step 3: 3 parts = £45, 5 parts = £75 ✓

Think of the ratio as coloured blocks:

3 parts = £45
5 parts = £75

Total = £120

Let the parts be 3x and 5x. Then 3x + 5x = 120 → 8x = 120 → x = 15. So 3x = 45 and 5x = 75.

3.3 Direct & Inverse Proportion

💡 Key Concept

Direct proportion: as one quantity increases, the other increases at the same rate. y = kx

Inverse proportion: as one increases, the other decreases. y = k/x

The Unitary Method

Find the value of ONE unit first, then multiply up.

Example: 5 books cost £40. Find the cost of 3 books.

1 book = £40 ÷ 5 = £8 → 3 books = 3 × £8 = £24

Proportion Graphs

Direct proportion gives a straight line through the origin. Inverse proportion gives a reciprocal curve.

3.4 Percentages

💡 Key Concept

Percentage means "per hundred". 25% = 25/100 = 0.25

Percentage of an Amount

% of amount = (percentage/100) × amount

Percentage Change

% change = (change / original) × 100

Using Multipliers

Increase by 15%: multiply by 1.15

Decrease by 20%: multiply by 0.80

Finding original after increase: divide by the multiplier

✏️ Worked Example — Reverse Percentage

After a 20% discount, a jacket costs £56. What was the original price?

Step 1: 20% off → multiplier = 0.80
Step 2: Original × 0.80 = £56
Step 3: Original = £56 ÷ 0.80 = £70 ✓

Compound Interest

Final = Original × (1 + r/100)ⁿ

✏️ Worked Example

£2000 is invested at 3% per year compound interest. Find the value after 4 years.

Step 1: Multiplier = 1.03
Step 2: Value = 2000 × 1.03⁴
Step 3: = 2000 × 1.12550881 ≈ £2251.02 ✓

3.5 Similarity & Scale Factors

💡 Key Concept

If the linear scale factor is k, then:

  • Lengths scale by k
  • Areas scale by k²
  • Volumes scale by k³

✏️ Worked Example

Two similar cones have heights 6 cm and 9 cm. If the smaller has volume 80 cm³, find the volume of the larger.

Step 1: Scale factor k = 9/6 = 1.5
Step 2: Volume scale factor = k³ = 1.5³ = 3.375
Step 3: Larger volume = 80 × 3.375 = 270 cm³ ✓

3.6 Inverse Proportion Equations

💡 Key Concept

If X is inversely proportional to Y, then X = k/Y (or equivalently, XY = k).

✏️ Worked Example

y is inversely proportional to x. When x = 4, y = 6. Find y when x = 8.

Step 1: y = k/x → 6 = k/4 → k = 24
Step 2: When x = 8: y = 24/8 = 3 ✓

3.7 Rates of Change Graphs

💡 Key Concept

The gradient of a straight-line graph represents the rate of change. For example, on a distance-time graph, the gradient = speed.

Interpreting Gradients on Curves Higher

For curves, draw a tangent at the point and find its gradient. This gives the instantaneous rate of change at that point.

3.8 Growth & Decay

💡 Key Concept

Exponential growth: y = a(1 + r)ⁿ (compound increase).

Exponential decay: y = a(1 − r)ⁿ (compound decrease).

✏️ Worked Example

A car worth £12 000 depreciates by 15% each year. What is it worth after 3 years?

Step 1: Multiplier = 1 − 0.15 = 0.85
Step 2: Value = £12 000 × 0.85³
Step 3: = £12 000 × 0.614125 = £7369.50 ✓
